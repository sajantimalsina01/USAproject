Step-by-Step Guide to Apply for US Universities

Click below to know about it:

  • Choose a major based on your interests and future career goals.
  • Talk to seniors or experts for counseling regarding course content and career scope.
a) English Proficiency Test:
  • IELTS
  • TOEFL
  • Duolingo English Test
  • PTE Academic
b) SAT (Optional but Beneficial):
  • Not mandatory for all universities.
  • Can significantly boost your scholarship chances.

Engage in extracurriculars such as clubs, volunteering, internships, sports, leadership roles, etc. A well-rounded profile increases admission and scholarship chances.

  • Essay / SOP: A strong statement of purpose improves admission chances.
  • Letters of Recommendation: From teachers, counselors, or mentors.
  • Identification Documents: Passport, Citizenship certificate
  • Academic Documents:
    • Grade 10 Mark Sheet
    • Grade 11 Transcript
    • Grade 12 Transcript
    • Provisional Certificate
    • Migration Certificate
  • Avoid common and low-ranked universities.
  • Look for better-ranked universities that match your profile.
  • Browse university ranking sites and forums for details.
a. Check Deadlines
  • Priority Deadline: For better chances and scholarships.
  • Regular Deadline: Final date to submit applications.
b. Start the Application
  1. Visit university website
  2. Find "Undergraduate Admission"
  3. Click "Apply Now"
  4. Create account using your email
  5. Fill in details and upload documents
  6. Submit application
  • Submit financial documents after acceptance
  • Receive I-20 form from the university
  • Fill DS-160 Form: Search on Google and fill it carefully
  • Create CGI Portal Account: Pay MRV fee ($185) and schedule interview
  • Pay SEVIS Fee: $350 after receiving I-20
  • Book Visa Interview: Via CGI portal. Prepare well for F-1 Visa interview
